This section introduces the three color palettes and when to use them.

  • Qualitative colors: There are 10 distinctly different colors that are used to different kinds or categories of data.
  • Significant colors: Use colors from this palette to indicate degrees, for example, risk levels (neutral, safe, low risk, medium risk, high risk).
  • Sequential colors: These are shades of a single color used to indicate different categories in a progression of one characteristic, for example, different population ranges (0 to 500, 501 to 1000, 1001 and more, etc.).
